### 📌 Project Executive Summary
This project features a comprehensive analysis of 10,000 customer records to solve the problem of increasing churn. By visualizing the relationship between credit scores, account balances, and activity status, I provided a data-backed framework for the bank to lower its churn rate toward the **15% target benchmark**.

### 🛠️ Technical Workflow & Tools
- **Power BI Desktop:** Used for end-to-end data visualization and dashboarding.
- **Data Modeling (Star Schema):** Built an efficient reporting structure to handle complex filtering across Geography and Demographics.
- **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions):** Created custom measures for:
  - `Churn Rate %`
  - `Retention vs. Attrition Targets`
  - `Active vs. Inactive Customer Ratios`
- **Power Query:** Grouped raw data into meaningful buckets like **Age Groups** and **Credit Score Tiers** to find hidden correlations.

### 📈 Strategic Business Insights
- **Demographic Risk:** Identified that the **41-50 age bracket** has the highest concentration of customers but also significant churn volatility.
- **Financial Thresholds:** Customers with account balances between **$100k - $200k** represent the most critical segment for high-value retention efforts.
- **Geographic Trends:** Analyzed churn variance across **France, Germany, and Spain**, identifying Germany as a key market for localized loyalty incentives.
- **Activity Correlation:** Data proves that "Active Members" are significantly less likely to churn, suggesting that a **5% increase in app engagement** could lower churn by [X]%.
